Definition: "Convergence alters the relationship between existing technologies, industries, markets, genres and audiences." -Jenkins

The Tetris film presents media convergence in its infancy. Specifically, the top-down corporate driven process. The race to acquire the licensing rights to Tetris involved numerous companies across 5 different countries: Soviet Union, Japan, United States, Germany, and the United Kingdom. The film explores the nuances of licensing intellectual property. The protagonist Henk Rogers flies to the Soviet Union to try and secure handheld rights after losing arcade rights to another company. There, he is accused of stealing the video game rights to Tetris because Elorg believes that the only version of Tetris they sold was for personal computers.

But, after looking at the contract, Henk discovers that Elorg did not properly define personal computers to have keyboards. Tetris is an example of media conglomerates working to spread intellectual property across multiple different media platforms.

The film itself is an example of franchising in modern day--a continuation of corporate strategies to expand the web of Tetris.

The film is also a great example of what Jenkins calls "Remapping Globalization." Jenkins believed that there was a focus on the flow of western media products into global media markets. However, he argued that globalization also involves the flow of media from the east to the west: I would argue that Tetris is one of the earliest media products to spearhead the flow of media from eastern to western markets.
